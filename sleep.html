Sleep

Are you having trouble sleeping?

Losing a night's sleep or even more can really afect our mind and energy levels the next day. Having a good sleep hygiene is as important as eating healthy and exercising. Getting a good night's sleep has many benefits, such as boosting your immunity, improving mental health and reducing stress. However, it can be a little difficult to fall asleep when we have so many distractions in the bedroom: television, tablets and phones, or maybe the mind is just overwhelmed. Whether you have regular or occcasional sleep issues you can learn how to better manage your sleep by introducing some new healthy habits into your daily routine.

Tips for a restful sleep

Practice being in the present moment throughout the day. Set an alarm and check in with your 5 senses. Ask yourself which are 3 things that you can see, hear, smell, touch and taste?
Stick to a sleep schedule- try to go to sleep and wake up at the same time every day. Being consistent tells your body when to be awake and when to be drowsy.
If your mind is racing at night, get your your thoughts on paper by journalling. Write things you are grateful for that happened during the day or in previous days.
We truly are what we eat. Try to avoid having stimulants before bed time, such as alcohol, sugar and caffeine. Also try having a balanced diet and proper hydration.
Go for a walk every day. When you are out look around you and try to be present. Remember checking in with your senses. Admire nature and be present. You will love it.
Make time in your day for exercise, even if it is just 30 minutes. Go for a walk, a bike ride or do a Yoga class. Any sort of physical activity helps us sleep better.
Avoid taking your devices to bed and limit screen time 2 hours before bedtime. Instead of scrolling, allow yourself to relax and process the day.
